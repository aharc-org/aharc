        <p>A New Reign</p>

        <p>by Adrian King</p>

        <p>This month we stand at the beginning of a New Reign. We will all be glued to our television screens to see the coronation of our new monarch - King Charles III.</p>

        <p>Some of us will be old enough to remember the last coronation seventy years ago! Others, like myself, have lived through Queen Elizabeths II reign, but were too young to remember the coronation! Family and friends would have been crowded around the small square wooden veneered box in the corner of the room of those who were fortunate enough to own a television set. The box was square because the screen was as deep as it was wide - no 60 inch flat screen televisions in those days!</p>

        <p>How have things changed in the village in the last seventy years? The answer will depend on the age of the person to which the question is directed! Even a young adult will have seen many changes, but not so many for a pupil in St. James First School.</p>

        <p>Back in January I was asked to visit St. James First School - their project for the term was “Village Detectives,” and I was asked to show old pictures of the village along side more recent ones of the same place, so that the young people could see how much things had changed - the presentation stimulated a lot of discussion for the rest of the day!</p>

        <p>Some discussion was about the difference now at Charing Cross. If you took a picture now and compared it with a picture that had been taken sixty years ago. It was not until the current picture was shown that the pupils could connect it to the present time.</p>

        <p>In the new picture the old railway bridge had been flattened and where at one time there had been fields, there was now Churchill Close. Today, the only building from sixty years ago is Charing Cottage!</p>

        <p>Up until the late 1960’s Alderholt was not a very healthy place to live. Most properties had a Septic Tank and they were beginning to leach into ground, and as many houses had wells the water was getting contaminated!</p>

        <p>The contractors, Whitehall Civil Engineering Company started installing main drainage in 1971 and was completed by 1973.
In 1973 a Village Plan for future development providing for an ultimate population of 2,400 was prepared and published.
Then the planning applications began to come in commencing in 1974. Hillbury Rise, (Windsor Way), Earlswood, Birchwood Park, Churchill Close, Silverdale and Wren Gardens were all built in phases. Wren Gardens was the last of the big estates to be completed in the 1990’s.</p>

        <p>Gas was first laid to the village in 1976 and the water supply was much improved. Some outlying areas of the parish did not have a reliable electric supply until the mid-1960’s.</p>

        <p>In 1971 the village had a population of 800. By 1981 this had risen to 1710 and by 1991 that had risen to 2880. Over the last 30 years it has stabilized around 3000 people.</p>

        <p>Communication has greatly improved - first we had telephones, a pair of twisted copper wires that could connect us to the rest of the world. Then came the mobile networks that reduced the use of the landline for voice communication.</p>

        <p>And now we have the internet - twenty years ago it was dial-up using a modem. We had blistering speeds of 56 kbps!</p>

        <p>And that was too slow, and then we had broadband - somehow they managed to piggyback the signal down the twisted copper pair with even faster speeds of 30 Mbps and higher in most of the village. I can now see my new granddaughter in New Zealand on my phone!</p>

        <p>Soon we will have fibre-optic, using light, with possible speeds of 1 Gbps plus!!</p>

	<p>Your Majesty, you have a majestic act to follow, but we wish you every blessing as you commence your reign.</p>
